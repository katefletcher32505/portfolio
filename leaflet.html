WRP Leaflet

A public-facing design project built around hierarchy, audience, information flow, and outcomes-focused communication.

About the Project

This leaflet was designed as a persuasive, user-facing communication piece for the Writing, Rhetoric, and Publication program. The goal was not just to present information, but to structure it in a way that felt clear, appealing, and easy to navigate.

The project required attention to hierarchy, pacing, visual balance, and audience expectations—especially how design choices influence whether information feels inviting, credible, or overwhelming.

What It Demonstrates

This piece reflects my interest in rhetoric not just as textual analysis, but as design in practice. It shows how communication decisions are shaped through layout, emphasis, sequence, and accessibility.

It also reflects revision-based thinking: how a project becomes stronger through restructuring, feedback, and closer attention to audience use.
