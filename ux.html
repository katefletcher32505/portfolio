UX Research

A usability case study focused on user behavior, trust signals, and friction points in digital navigation.

About the Project

This project used usability testing and observation to examine how users moved through a digital environment, where they hesitated, and what features increased or weakened trust. Rather than treating design as purely visual, the project focused on how people actually interpret structure, clarity, and credibility in real time.

It was especially useful for thinking through friction: what slows users down, what makes them uncertain, and what causes them to question whether a site feels legitimate or usable.

What It Demonstrates

This work demonstrates audience awareness, applied research skills, and the ability to turn observation into design insight. It reflects my broader interest in the overlap between psychology, rhetoric, usability, and communication strategy.

It also shows how trust is not only a matter of content, but of interface, expectation, and user experience.
